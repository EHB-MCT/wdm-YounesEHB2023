## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

**‚úÖ Positive Behaviors We Encourage:**

- **Respect and Kindness**: Treat others with respect, kindness, and empathy
- **Inclusive Language**: Use inclusive language and be mindful of cultural differences
- **Constructive Feedback**: Provide constructive, helpful feedback that focuses on the code, not the person
- **Patience with Learners**: Be patient and supportive with beginners and those learning new concepts
- **Collaboration**: Work together and help others when possible
- **Open Communication**: Communicate openly and honestly about challenges and needs
- **Professionalism**: Maintain professional behavior in all interactions
- **Learning Mindset**: Approach discussions with a willingness to learn and grow

**üéì Educational Environment Standards:**

- **Supportive Learning**: Create a safe space for asking questions and making mistakes
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Share knowledge generously and acknowledge different learning styles
- **Mentorship**: Actively mentor and guide less experienced contributors
- **Curiosity and Exploration**: Encourage curiosity and exploration of new concepts
- **Academic Integrity**: Uphold academic honesty and proper attribution

### Unacceptable Behavior

**‚ùå Behaviors We Don't Tolerate:**

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, sexual language, or imagery
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory comments, jokes, or behavior based on identity or background
- **Personal Attacks**: Insults, personal attacks, or ad hominem arguments
- **Spam**: Unwelcome content, repeated off-topic messages, or unsolicited commercial content
- **Disruptive Behavior**: Trolling, deliberate provocation, or repeated disruption of discussions
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing others' private information without permission
- **Intimidation**: Threats or intimidation of any kind
- **Academic Dishonesty**: Plagiarism, cheating, or other forms of academic misconduct

**üö´ Specific Examples:**

- Using sexualized language or imagery
- Making jokes about disabilities, characteristics, or background
- Publicly shaming or embarrassing someone for their questions or mistakes
- Deliberately providing incorrect or harmful information
- Taking credit for others' work without attribution
- Creating a hostile or unwelcoming environment

## üõ†Ô∏è Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report Issues

**If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:**

1. **Direct Response** (if you feel comfortable):
   - Politely but firmly address the behavior
   - Ask the person to stop the problematic behavior
   - Remove yourself from the situation if needed

2. **Report to Maintainers**:
   - Email: younes.ben.ali@student.ehb.be
   - GitHub Issues: Create a private issue with [CONDUCT] prefix
   - Direct Message: Contact maintainers through appropriate channels

3. **Information to Include**:
   - What happened (be specific)
   - When and where it occurred
   - Who was involved (if known)
   - Any witnesses
   - Screenshots or logs (if applicable and available)

### Reporting Process

**Step 1: Initial Report**
- Submit report through appropriate channel
- Include as much detail as possible
- Specify if you want anonymity

**Step 2: Review**
- Maintainers will review within 48 hours
- Initial response and assessment of urgency
- Immediate action for serious violations

**Step 3: Investigation**
- Gather information from all parties
- Interview witnesses if necessary
- Review logs, messages, or other evidence

**Step 4: Resolution**
- Determine appropriate actions
- Communicate outcome to reporter
- Implement changes to prevent future incidents

### Confidentiality
- All reports will be kept confidential
- Information shared only with necessary parties
- Anonymity respected when requested
- No retaliation for good-faith reports

## üåç Community Guidelines

### Communication Standards

**‚úÖ Do:**
- Assume good faith intentions
- Ask questions respectfully
- Listen actively to others' perspectives
- Admit mistakes and learn from them
- Celebrate others' successes and learning
- Use inclusive and welcoming language
- Take breaks when discussions become heated

**‚ùå Don't:**
- Make assumptions about others' knowledge or experience
- Use sarcasm or jokes that could be misunderstood
- Interrupt others while they're speaking
- Dominate conversations or exclude others
- Use excessive technical jargon without explanation
- Make snap judgments about others' abilities

### Learning Environment

**Creating a Safe Learning Space:**
- Welcome questions from all skill levels
- Celebrate learning milestones and progress
- Provide resources and references for further learning
- Acknowledge different learning styles and paces
- Create opportunities for hands-on practice
- Encourage peer-to-peer teaching and mentoring

### Cultural Sensitivity

**International Community:**
- Be aware of time zones when scheduling discussions
- Use clear, simple English for international contributors
- Be patient with language barriers
- Respect different cultural norms and holidays
- Avoid idioms that don't translate well
- Be understanding of different communication styles

## üíª Technical Community Guidelines

### Code Review Standards

**‚úÖ Constructive Code Reviews:**
- Focus on code quality, not person
- Explain why changes are needed
- Provide examples or resources for learning
- Acknowledge good parts of the code
- Offer to help implement suggestions
- Be patient with different skill levels

**‚ùå Avoid in Code Reviews:**
- Personal criticism or attacks
- Sarcastic or dismissive comments
- Overwhelming amount of feedback at once
- Ignoring the original author's intent
- Making assumptions about knowledge level

### Technical Discussions

**Productive Technical Conversations:**
- Base arguments on evidence and best practices
- Acknowledge when you don't know something
- Be open to different approaches and solutions
- Provide context for technical decisions
- Share learning resources and references
- Respect different levels of expertise

## üéØ Project-Specific Guidelines

### Educational Project Standards

**As an Educational Project, We Emphasize:**
- **Learning Over Perfection**: Focus on educational value and growth
- **Mentorship and Support**: Actively help others learn and improve
- **Documentation**: Provide clear explanations for complex concepts
- **Examples**: Include working examples to illustrate concepts
- **Feedback**: Constructive feedback that helps people grow

**Student Contributors:**
- Feel free to ask "basic" questions
- It's okay to make mistakes and learn from them
- Share what you're learning with others
- Don't be afraid to try new approaches
- Your learning journey is valuable to the community

**Experienced Contributors:**
- Mentor and guide less experienced contributors
- Share best practices and industry insights
- Be patient and supportive with questions
- Create learning opportunities through your contributions
- Remember what it was like when you were learning

### Academic Integrity

**Upholding Academic Standards:**
- Give proper attribution for borrowed code
- Cite sources for algorithms and techniques
- Don't claim others' work as your own
- Be transparent about AI assistance or external help
- Follow institutional academic honesty policies
- Document collaborative work appropriately
